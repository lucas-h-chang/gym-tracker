Privacy Policy

BearMeter — Last updated June 11, 2026

BearMeter is a gym capacity tracker for the RSF at UC Berkeley. This policy explains what data the app collects and how it is used.

What we collect

  • Push notification token. If you enable notifications, your device generates an anonymous token that Apple assigns to your device. We store this token in order to send you alerts about gym capacity.

We do not collect your name, email address, location, health data, or any other personal information.

How we use it

Your device token is used solely to deliver push notifications you have opted into — for example, alerts when the RSF reaches a capacity threshold you selected. We do not use it for advertising or analytics, and we do not sell or share it with third parties.

Data storage

Device tokens are stored in a secure database (Supabase). They are retained as long as you have notifications enabled and are deleted when the app can no longer reach your device.

How to opt out

You can stop notifications at any time by going to iOS Settings → Notifications → BearMeter and turning them off. Your token will no longer receive any messages.

Third-party services

  • Supabase — database and backend

Children

BearMeter is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect data from them.

Changes

If this policy changes materially, we will update the date at the top of this page.
